Pav Bhaji

  • Preparation Time: 15 minutes
  • Cooking Time: 35 minutes
  • Serves: 4
  • Ingredients
    • To boil vegetables
      • 200 gms Cauliflower florets
      • 1 cup Carrot, Peeled, cubes
      • 1 capsicum, chopped
      • 1 cup Green Peas
      • Salt to taste
    • For Bhaji
      • 1 tbsp Oil
      • 100 gms Butter (add gradually)
      • 1 tsp Cumin Seeds
      • 1 tbsp Ginger Garlic paste
      • 2 Medium Onions, finely chopped
      • 4 Large Tomatoes, finely chopped
      • 3 Medium Potatoes, boiled & peeled
      • 1 tbsp Kashmiri Red Chili powder
      • 1 tsp Turmeric Powder
      • 3 tbsp Pav Bhaji Masala
      • Salt to taste
      • 1 no lime juice
      • 3 tbsp Fresh Coriander, chopped
    • For Pav

    • For Serving
      • 1 Large Onion, finely chopped
      • 1 Lemon, cut into quarters
      • 1 tbsp Butter
  • Process
    • For Boiled Vegetables
      • Add cauliflower, carrots, green peas and capsicum in pressure cooker.
      • Now add water just to cover the vegetables.
      • Give 5 whistles.
      • Make sure vegetables gets mushy & porridge kind of texture.
      • Add cooked potatoes and mash well.
    • For Bhaji
      • On a large tawa or kadhai heat oil and butter on medium flame.
      • Add onions and saute for a minute.
      • Add ginger garlic paste and saute till onion is golden in colour.
      • Add in tomatoes and cook for 3-4 mins while stirring continuously.
      • Add little amount of butter, mix well and cook for 3-4 minutes.
      • Add potatoes and mash them along with boiled vegetables using a potato masher.
      • Add kashmiri red chilli to the bhaji and mix well.
      • Add water little by little for that perfect consistency.
      • Add turmeric powder and mix well. Add some more butter and mix well.
      • Add pav bhaji masala in two parts, half while cooking and half when the bhaji is cooked.
      • Add the cooked and mashed vegetables and mix well.
      • Add butter and salt, mix well.
      • Add some more water and cook for 5-6 minutes.
      • Finish with lime juice & chopped fresh coriander leaves.
    • For Pav
      • Heat a tbsp of butter and add little red chili powder on a tawa.
      • Dab 2 pavs at a time and toast them as desired. Repeat till all the pavs are toasted.
    • For Serving
      • Portion out bhaji on plate and put a dollop of butter on top.
      • Place onion and lemon on side.
      • Garnish with fresh coriander and serve along with pav.
  • Note
    • You can cook potatoes along with the other vegetables to instead of cooking separately.
    • You can reduce the quantity of butter as per your preference.
